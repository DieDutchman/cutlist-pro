# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cabinet makers, joiners, and woodworking business owners — used both in the workshop (tablet/laptop mid-project) and at the desk (quoting, invoicing, supplier management). Users are skilled tradespeople, not software natives. They need speed and accuracy, not hand-holding. Some users are supplier-side (managing material pricing and distributing jobs to clients).

## Product Purpose

CutList Pro is a quoting, estimation, and job management tool for the cabinet and joinery trade. It calculates cut lists, optimises sheet usage, generates professional quotes with scenario options, tracks job costs, and manages supplier pricing. Success looks like a tradesperson going from a site measurement to a signed quote in minutes, not hours — with confidence the numbers are right.

## Brand Personality

Precise, efficient, professional.

The app should feel like the best tool in a skilled tradesperson's kit: no fuss, no flash, but unmistakably high-quality. It earns trust through accuracy and speed, not through visual theatre.

## Anti-references

- **Old-school trade software** (Excel-like, Access database UIs): dense grids, no visual hierarchy, zero craft in the UI. CutList Pro is premium, not legacy.
- **Over-designed / flashy**: heavy animations, gradient-heavy hero sections, decorative elements that slow the user down. Design serves the work, not itself.
- Generic SaaS cream/white aesthetics (Notion, Linear) are a secondary risk — the dark amber theme already avoids this, but it's worth preserving.

## Design Principles

1. **Data is the hero.** Numbers, dimensions, and costs are the product. Typography and layout frame them; they never compete with them.
2. **Precision over decoration.** Every element earns its place. If it doesn't help the user get the job done faster or more accurately, it shouldn't be there.
3. **Workshop-tested clarity.** UI must be readable on a tablet in a dusty workshop as well as on a widescreen at a desk. Obvious, fast, unambiguous — gloves-on usable.
4. **Premium without pretense.** Feels like professional-grade software. Not a startup, not a consumer app, not a legacy tool. Quiet confidence.
5. **Trust through consistency.** Tradespeople rely on the numbers being right. The UI must feel bulletproof — stable layout, predictable interactions, no surprises.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target: WCAG AA minimum.
- Dark theme is the default; a warm light theme is available (supplier/user-light). Both themes must meet contrast requirements independently.
- Touch targets must work on tablets (workshop use case).
- No reliance on colour alone to convey status — use icons or labels alongside colour cues.
