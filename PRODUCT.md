# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cabinet makers, kitchen designers, and shop owners based in South Africa. Primary user is a working tradesperson who quotes jobs on-site or in a workshop environment, often on a laptop or desktop with a keyboard. Secondary users include shop owners reviewing P&L and suppliers entering board/edge pricing. Users are domain-fluent: they know what a "F600 Floor Unit" means, understand edging codes, and think in millimetres. They are not SaaS consumers — they are tradespeople who want speed and precision over guidance and handholding.

**Context of use**: A job quoting session typically involves selecting units across multiple rooms, setting quantities, reviewing material costs against a supplier's price list, and generating a cutlist for the workshop. Sessions can be interrupted. Speed matters more than discovery.

## Product Purpose

CutList Pro generates accurate cabinet cutlists and client quotes for kitchen and joinery jobs. It takes a room-by-room unit selection, calculates part dimensions per unit, consolidates materials for a shopping list, and produces client-facing quote documents with markup applied. Success looks like: a cabinet maker can quote a full kitchen — with accurate parts list, material costs, and client price — in under 15 minutes.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Trade-grade. No-nonsense.

The tool should feel like a well-worn workshop instrument: no decoration that doesn't earn its place, no softening language, no consumer-SaaS friendliness. Think: a digital version of a well-organised job folder, not a startup's onboarding flow.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (Salesforce, HubSpot aesthetics)
- Material Design clones with rounded cards and light drop shadows
- Consumer app aesthetics (pastel palettes, illustration-heavy empty states, friendly copy)
- "Modern web app" defaults: Inter + white background + blue primary button
- Dribbble-ready dashboards with hero metrics and gradient cards

## Design Principles

1. **Precision over decoration.** Every visual element must earn its place. Numbers, labels, and relationships are the product — not the chrome around them.
2. **Trade fluency assumed.** Users know their domain. Labels use trade language (edging codes, unit IDs, sheet sizes) without explanation. No dumbing down.
3. **Dense, not cluttered.** The interface can carry a lot of data. Density is a feature when hierarchy is clear. Clutter is the failure mode, not density itself.
4. **Speed is UX.** A cabinet maker quoting on-site does not want to hunt. Primary actions should be immediate; secondary actions should be one gesture away.
5. **Confidence at every step.** Users commit real money to quotes. The interface must communicate certainty: clear totals, visible material sources, explicit confirmation on destructive actions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target: WCAG AA on contrast ratios. Keyboard navigation for primary flows. No motion requirements beyond standard browser support. The app is desktop-primary; mobile is a secondary screen for review, not for data entry.
